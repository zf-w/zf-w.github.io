Introduction

This part of my website is about some of my thoughts on games in general, including game solving, game design, cognitive science with board games. The project started with solving Connect Four, but later, I feel it would be more natural to extract the game solving techniques and concepts out.

Learning to Solve Connect Four

I have been thinking about learning to solve classic board games like Connect 4 and Game of Go for a long time. Finally, in the winter holiday of 2024, I have some time to study into Connect Four Solving. By studying the blog http://blog.gamesolver.org, I have implemented the game solver, calculated the opening book, and combined it with a server backend and frontend webpage.

Here is the link to the demo.

General Game Solving

After I, hopefully, successfully first reproduced and then replicated the result of the game solving blog mentioned above. I have been thinking about how to generalize the design and make dependencies between different concepts and objects clearer. I think I need to seperate the concepts and designs of game, game scoring and heuristics, game solver, and results memorizations. There are many things I need to think about, for example: whether I need to allow "runtime" games, in contrast to "complie time" games, like, if a Connect Four game need to have different board sizes at runtime.

Overall Reflection

This project of solving Connect Four is a huge step for me. I made a Connect-Four solving library, used it to generate opening books for speeding up the calculation, and built and deployed a web-server with an front-end user interface.

This project is a bridge, connecting the two seemingly very different areas in computer science in my mind. Months ago, my CS world was basicly seperated in two parts: the theoretical part which is about data structures, time and space complexities, algorithms, UIUC CS124~225 Machine Problems, and Online Judges, and the engineering part which is about webpages, servers serving static files, port numbers, requests, responses, and applications. During the project, I feel the two continents collided and merged into one.

Now, I have started to see the merits of the Cambridge AL Computer Science course design. I used to think there was a huge mind gap between how routers work and the time complexity of a binary tree. Now I feel that maybe, deep down, they are all solutions that are communicating and bound by the real world. Although I said I was respecting the contents of AL CS about how a laser printer works, I was probably not. I should probably apologize to my high school CS teacher. Although I didn't subjectively underrate the AL CS content, I might still have expressed a certain attitude to some extent.
